Administrative Judge Ethics Complaint Triage Report

1. Preliminary result

{{State exactly one preliminary result: can_proceed, cannot_proceed, redirect_to_appeal_or_other_forum, or insufficient_information. Then give a short 1–2 sentence explanation of what that result means in plain language.}}

2. Short answer

{{Give a plain-language answer in 2–4 sentences. Explain whether the situation appears to fit the Conseil de la justice administrative ethics complaint process, whether it appears outside scope, whether it should be redirected to appeal or another forum, or whether more information is needed. Use careful language such as “appears to,” “may,” and “based on the information provided.”}}

3. CJA jurisdiction

{{Explain whether the tribunal or body involved appears to be within the Conseil de la justice administrative’s competence. Mention the specific tribunal or body if known. If the tribunal is outside scope, explain that clearly. If the tribunal is unclear, say what information is missing.}}

4. Decision status and connection to the matter

{{Summarize whether a decision was rendered and whether the user was a party or otherwise connected to the matter. Explain why this matters for the triage result. If these facts are missing or unclear, list them clearly.}}

5. User objective

{{Explain what the user appears to want. Classify the objective as one or more of the following: professional discipline or ethics review; appeal or change of decision; damages or compensation; new hearing; fine or punishment of another party; unclear. Explain whether this objective fits the CJA ethics complaint process.}}

6. Conduct complained of

{{Neutrally summarize the specific conduct described by the user. Focus on words, actions, behaviour, communications, delay, bias, conflict of interest, abuse of status, or other conduct. Do not exaggerate the facts. If the user mainly disagrees with the decision and does not describe conduct, say so clearly.}}

7. Ethics issue classification

{{Classify the alleged conduct into relevant ethics categories. For each category, provide:





category name;



confidence level: low, medium, or high;



short reason.

Possible categories include disrespectful conduct, apparent bias, unequal treatment, improper assistance to one party, improper communication with one party in the absence of the other, conflict of interest, lack of diligence, unreasonable delay, abuse of status, improper use of tribunal resources, incompatible outside activities, political activity, or outside-work conduct affecting public confidence.}}

8. Why this result was reached

{{Explain the reasoning behind the preliminary result. Clearly distinguish between an ethics complaint and an appeal. If the complaint may proceed, explain why the issue is about conduct rather than simply the outcome. If the matter should be redirected, explain why the CJA is not the right forum. If information is insufficient, explain which missing facts prevent classification.}}

9. Strengths and weaknesses of the complaint

{{If the preliminary result is can_proceed, identify the strongest aspects of the potential complaint and any weaker or evidentiary issues. If the result is cannot_proceed or redirect_to_appeal_or_other_forum, explain the main weakness that prevents the complaint from fitting the CJA process. If insufficient_information, explain what facts are most important to clarify.}}

10. Missing facts

{{List any missing or unclear facts that would help assess or prepare the complaint. If the available facts are enough for preliminary triage but more details are needed for filing, say: “The available facts are sufficient for preliminary triage, but the following filing details should still be confirmed.” Include practical filing details such as judge name, file number, exact date, exact words used, whether the hearing was recorded, whether a transcript exists, witnesses, and available documents.}}

11. Evidence checklist

{{Provide a practical checklist of evidence the user should gather. Include only evidence relevant to the ethics complaint, not materials used only to reargue the merits of the decision. Possible items include:





decision;



tribunal file number;



notice of hearing;



transcript or recording, if available;



emails or letters;



screenshots;



notes from the hearing;



witness names;



proof of date and context of the conduct;



chronology of events.}}

12. Things the CJA likely cannot do

{{Explain, where relevant, that the CJA generally does not change or overturn the decision, award damages, impose a fine for the user’s benefit, order a new hearing, replace the judge for the original matter, or punish the opposing party. If the user is not asking for these remedies, state that this supports treating the matter as an ethics triage rather than an appeal request.}}

13. Action items

{{Provide practical action items tailored to the preliminary result.

If can_proceed, include filing-preparation steps: identify the judge, prepare chronology, gather evidence, separate conduct from merits, describe the requested disciplinary or corrective measure, and avoid asking the CJA to change the decision.

If cannot_proceed, explain what the user should do instead, such as identifying the correct institution or waiting for a decision if no decision exists.

If redirect_to_appeal_or_other_forum, suggest checking appeal or review deadlines, obtaining the decision, identifying the correct appeal/review body, and keeping any ethics issue separate from appeal arguments.

If insufficient_information, list the facts the user should collect before using the tool again or before deciding whether to file.}}

14. Disclaimer

This tool provides legal information and triage support only. It does not provide legal advice, decide admissibility, or file a complaint for you.
