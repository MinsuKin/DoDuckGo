I want to file an ethics complaint against an administrative judge.

1. Tribunal or body involved:
Tribunal administratif du logement.

2. Was a decision rendered?
Yes. A written decision was rendered on March 14, 2025.

3. Was I a party to the matter?
Yes. I was the tenant and the applicant in the TAL file.

4. What was the case about?
The case was about my landlord’s refusal to make urgent repairs and my request for a rent reduction because the heating system was not working properly during winter.

5. What do I want from the Conseil de la justice administrative?
I am not asking the CJA to change the TAL decision. I understand the CJA is not an appeal body. I want the administrative judge’s conduct to be reviewed because I believe the judge behaved in a way that was disrespectful, partial, and incompatible with the dignity and impartiality expected from an administrative judge. I am seeking a disciplinary or corrective response, such as an investigation, reprimand, suspension, or other appropriate sanction.

6. Am I trying to change, overturn, appeal, or review the decision?
No. I may separately look into appeal or review options, but this complaint is about the judge’s conduct during the hearing, not about changing the result of the decision.

7. Am I seeking damages, money compensation, a fine, a new hearing, or a different judge?
No. I am not asking the CJA for damages, a fine, a new hearing, or a different judge. I am asking for an ethics review of the administrative judge’s conduct.

8. Did the behaviour happen during the administrative decision-making process?
Yes. The behaviour happened during the hearing and also in the way the hearing was conducted.

9. Did the behaviour happen outside the administrative decision-making process?
No, or at least I am not aware of any outside-work behaviour. My concern is about what happened during the hearing.

10. Description of the conduct:
During the hearing, the administrative judge repeatedly interrupted me when I tried to explain the heating problem and the evidence I had submitted. The judge said, in a sarcastic tone, “tenants always exaggerate these things,” and told me that I was “wasting everyone’s time” before I had finished presenting my evidence.

The judge allowed the landlord to speak for long periods without interruption, but interrupted me several times when I tried to respond. When I tried to refer to photos showing the temperature inside the apartment and emails I had sent to the landlord, the judge said that “photos can be staged” and moved on without letting me explain them.

At one point, the landlord started explaining what legal arguments I should have made. The judge then appeared to help the landlord by saying, “what you probably mean is that there was no formal notice,” and allowed the landlord to adopt that argument. I felt the judge was giving legal assistance to the landlord.

There was also a moment during a break where I saw the judge and the landlord’s representative speaking in the hearing room while I was outside. I could not hear the full conversation, but when I came back in, they stopped talking. I was not told what the conversation was about. This made me concerned that there may have been communication with one party in the absence of the other.

11. Date and context of the conduct:
The hearing took place on February 20, 2025, by videoconference. The conduct happened during the hearing and during a short break in the hearing.

12. Why I think this is an ethics issue:
I believe this is not just disagreement with the decision. My concern is that the judge may have failed to act with dignity, impartiality, and fairness. The judge made disrespectful comments, appeared to treat me differently from the landlord, may have helped the landlord formulate a legal argument, and may have communicated with the landlord’s representative while I was absent.

13. Available evidence:
I have the TAL decision dated March 14, 2025. I have the notice of hearing. I have copies of the photos, emails, and documents I tried to rely on during the hearing. I also made detailed notes immediately after the hearing describing the comments and interruptions. I do not currently have an official transcript or recording, but I would like to know whether I should try to obtain one.

14. Witnesses:
No independent witness was present with me, but the landlord and the landlord’s representative were present during the hearing. The hearing was by videoconference.

15. What I want help with:
Please assess whether this situation appears to be a potential ethics complaint within the Conseil de la justice administrative’s process. If it may proceed, please give me a structured report, classify the ethics issues, identify missing facts, list evidence I should gather, and provide practical action items before filing the complaint.
